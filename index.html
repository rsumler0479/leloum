How Lelou Solidarity Inc. Will Achieve Its Mission

To achieve its mission , Lelou will focus on solving important social issues by:

  1. Providing social & emotional assistance to vulnerable members, such as the What We Doless, the elderly, or people with disabilities within the Lelouma community.

  2. Promoting health and well-being, such as through disease prevention, mental health services.

  3. Advancing education, such as by providing scholarships, or creating educational programs to teach Fulani to members and the community children.

  4. Supporting the arts and culture of Guinea, by having cultural activities to promote Fula cultures.

The Benefits of Joining Lelou Solidarity Inc.

Only active members of Lelou Solidarity Inc. that are in good standing with the association will enjoy these benefits.

  1. Death:
    The family of an active member who passed away will receive $1000.00 after the burial; The deceased membership dues must be current at the time of death. If the balance is not current, the family members will be allowed to pay any balance owed for up to 3 months.

  2. Sickness:
    - Any active member incapacitated for a month due to a serious condition or disease, and who cannot work because of his/her sickness will receive $500.00. The active member must provide proof of his/her condition to the executive team.
    - Any sick member in good standing with the association at the time of sickness can have his membership fees suspended or forgiven for the length of time he/she is incapacitated. Such decision will be made by the executive team.

  3. Immigration and other legal matters:
    Active members dealing with serious immigration or legal matters issues can request help from the association, if arrested.
    The association will provide moral support and can decide to assist financially the member for up to $1000.00. The decision will have to be made by the executive board. The applicant must provide documentation and proof of his/her situation to the Board before a decision can be made. The applicant must be in good standing with the association.

  4. Wedding/Childbirth/Name Ceremony:
    The secretary of communication must notify all members of the association via the different channels of communication available to the association, of the wedding, childbirth, and naming ceremony of any active member of the association in good standing with Lelouma United. Members are free to participate to any potential fund raising establish in the name of the member.

Lelou Solidarity Inc's Long Term Vision For The Future:


One of the long-term projects that the association plan to accomplish is to acquire a building to serve as a community center. The community center will be the focal point that will be the gathering place for all social, cultural, educational et recreational activities for members.

  1. Why does Lelou need a community center ?
    Lelouma United community center will offer a wide range of services to meet the needs of the members. Some of the services will generally be offered for a small fee for the members and full price for non-members to generate revenue that will be reinvested in social programs. The community center should have space for the following:

    • Headquarter's
    • Meeting and training rooms for workshops, classes,
    • Daycare and after school programs facility
    • Laundromats open to the public.
    • Parking lot/garage

  2. Where would this be developed ?
    The selection of the location of the Lelouma United Community Center will be determined based on the needs of the community, how easily accessible to the members, how much resources will be necessary to acquire it, if the location will meet zoning regulations, and where it will be safe and secure.

    • Demographics and Needs: The demographics and needs of the community will be crucial when choosing the location for the community center. The state and area with the highest number of members from Lelouma United and people from Guinea will be a determining factor for the selection of the location. Age and income will also have to be considered to determine the type of services and programs that will be most useful and effective.
    • Accessibility: The Community center will be located in a central area that is easily accessible by public transportation or car, and should be close to schools, parks, and other community resources.
    • Available Resources: The community center will be rented or bought with the association resources. Volunteers, and community support will also be considered along with the availability of resources in the area when choosing a location for the center.
    • Zoning Regulations: Zoning regulations may affect the location of a community center, Lelouma United will do its due diligence to understand the regulations of an area before selecting a location.
    • Safety and Security: Safety and security will be important considerations when choosing a location for the Lelouma United community center. The organization will look for a location that is well-lit, has good visibility, and is located in a low-crime area.

  3. What are the requirements to develop the center ?
    Developing a community center requires careful planning, resources, and a clear understanding of the needs of the community.

    • Identify the Needs of the Community: Conduct a needs assessment to identify the needs and priorities of the community. This will help determine the types of programs and services that the community center should offer.
    • Create a Business Plan: Develop a business plan that outlines the mission, goals, objectives, and strategies for the community center. This plan should also include a budget, staffing requirements, and a timeline for implementation.
    • Secure Funding: Determine the funding sources for the community center, including grants, donations, and other fundraising initiatives. Consider partnering with local businesses or organizations to secure additional support.
    • Select a Location: The association will choose a location that is easily accessible and meets the needs of the community and will consider factors such as proximity to public transportation, schools, parks, and other community resources.
    • Recruit Staff and Volunteers: Hire qualified staff and recruit volunteers to support the operations of the community center. This may include program coordinators, administrative staff, and volunteers for events and activities.
    • Develop Programs and Services: Based on the needs assessment, develop programs and services that address the needs and interests of the community. This may include educational programs, recreational activities, and support services.
    • Establish Partnerships: The association intend to collaborate with local businesses, government agencies, and community organizations to establish partnerships and build support for the community center.
    • Promote the Community Center: Develop a marketing plan to promote the community center and its programs to the community. This may include social media, flyers, and other promotional materials.

    Lelouma United should consider working with another non-profit organization to acquire a community center:

    • Shared Resources: By working with another non-profit organization, Lelouma United can share resources, expertise, and funding sources, which can reduce costs and increase efficiency.
    • Increased Impact: By combining forces with another non-profit organization, Lelouma United can increase its collective impact on the community.     • Risk Mitigation: Acquiring and managing a community center can be a significant financial and operational risk for a non-profit organization.     • Pros:
      The community center will serve as a hub for various social, cultural, educational, and recreational activities. It will help to strengthen community ties, foster a sense of belonging and connection and serve as a resource center, providing access to information, services, and support that members might not otherwise have access to.
